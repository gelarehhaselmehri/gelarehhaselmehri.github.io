## In short

I am a PhD candidate in Information Security at [Eindhoven University of Technology](https://www.tue.nl), graduating in October 2026. I work under the supervision of [Dr. Nicola Zannone](https://research.tue.nl/en/persons/nicola-zannone) on advancing data protection through complex access control systems.

My research focuses on making access control systems more secure, understandable, and user-centric. I tackle three interconnected challenges in modern security

Data protection in collaborative environments where multiple stakeholders need to share resources securely
Authentication anomaly detection to prevent unauthorized access and privilege misuse
Explainability and transparency so that access control decisions can be understood and justified to all parties involved

My work bridges theory and practice, combining game theory, machine learning, and causal reasoning to develop frameworks that protect data while respecting user privacy and organizational needs. My contributions have been recognized by leading venues in the field, including ACM SACMAT and the Microsoft Bounty Program.

## Research areas

**Explainable Access Control** - Developing frameworks to help administrators, stakeholders, and users understand why access decisions are made. This includes causal reasoning approaches and visualization techniques for policy evaluation.

**Anomaly Detection for Access Control** - Building machine learning models that profile normal user authentication behavior and detect deviations that may indicate privilege misuse or compromise.

**Privacy-Preserving Collaborative Access** - Designing systems where multiple parties can control shared resources according to their privacy preferences, using game-theoretic modeling and deep learning techniques.

**Advanced Access Control Models** - Working on access decision-making in complex settings including online social networks, cloud environments, and healthcare systems.
